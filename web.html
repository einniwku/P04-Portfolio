Shattered

This webpage was made for the project I did for IAT 337 in SFU. Shattered is a kinectic sculpture that gives off the perception of glass shattering when energy is consumed. The site is meant to let people understand what the sculpture does and the process we went through to create the final piece.

With the material we chose for our project to be white and clear acrylic, it demonstrates a very clean feel to it. I wanted the website to be compatible with the final product itself, so I kept the site simple and chose white background with black writings. This way, the pictures in the site stands out better as well.

Since the site was meant to keep a record of our making process, the style of the site was "blog". Hierarchy is very important to blogs as headings are the main thing that users look for when reading a blog; they would want to read the sections that they are interested in.

There are quite a bit of picutres in the site as we used pictures to record the process. To see the details of the pictures, users can click on the picture to zoom in and have a better view of the picture.

The navigation is kept simple listing out all the pages in the site. The color of the tab changes as user go into different pages to let the user know where they are on the page. In the mobile version, the navigation bar is collapsed into a menu which drops down when user clicks to navigate through the site.

This website is responsive to desktop and mobile devices. For more details and to explore around the site, please visit:
